  10. Discussion and Open Forum
    1. Personal Growth and Character Development

      2. Five high school students from different walks of life endure a Saturday detention under a power-hungry principal. The disparate
      group includes rebel John, princess Claire, outcast Allison, brainy Brian and Andrew, the jock. Each has a chance to tell his
      or her story, making the others see them a little differently -- and when the day ends, they question whether school will ever
      be the same.

    2. Character Conflicts

      Throughout the film, the different characters are in conflict with each other -- mostly because they come from different social
      & economic groups. For example, the verbal and psychological conflict between John and Claire is the result of their very
      different class backgrounds. Another important conflict shown in the film is that between John and the teacher responsible for
      detention. An underlying conflict is that between each of the students and their parents.

    3. Character Cooperation

      The interaction between the five members of the Breakfast Club is full of examples of cooperation. The five work together to sneak through
      the hallways and avoid detection. They try to cover for John after he returns to the library via the ceiling passageway. Brian agrees to
      write the paper to represent the other four members of the group. The dance scenes near the end of the movie are also examples of cooperation
      - as the students work together to have fun and blow off steam after an emotionally draining day of detention.
